# final exam review
Q>What are the six fundamental coordinate transformations in computer graphs?
A>model/object/world -> eye -> clip -> NDC -> window

Q>What are the vectors and points used in calculating the Phong illumination model?
A> light vector, normal vector, view vector, reflection (of light vector across normal), halfway vector (between light vector and view vector).

Q>Which Phong illumination model property is based on viewer's position?
A>Specular.

Q>Which Phong illumination model property is based on surface normal?
A>Diffuse.

Q>What is the maximum value of the cosine function from -pi to pi?
A>Max value is 1, min value is -1.

Q>What is the shininess coefficient in the Phong model?
A>Shininess sharpens the specular highlight.

Q>If a red ball is illuminated by a blue light, what color will the Phong model render the ball?
A>A black ball, [1,0,0]*[0,0,1] = [0,0,0].

Q>Flat shading.
A>Light/color is computed per triangle.

Q>Gouraud shading.
A>Interpolate the color at each vertex aka vertex shading.

Q>Phong shading.
A>AKA fragment shading, give algorithms points and normals at the vertices and compute phong at each fragment normal.

Q>What is per-fragment lighting?
A>Phong shading.

Q>Give a triangle with vertices colored red, green and blue what is the smooth shaded color at the centroid?
A>[1/3, 1/3, 1/3] which is Grey.

Q>A surface of revolution s(v, theta) is defined by generating curve g(v) = [v^2,0,v] for v=[0,1] and a rotation around the y-axis. What is the parametric equation for s(v, theta)?
A>s(v, theta) = [v^2*cos(theta),v,v^2*sin(theta)]

Q>How is the surface normal for s(v, theta) calculated?
A>partial of s wrt v cross partial of theta wrt theta / || *** || normalized vector.

Q>What is backface culling?
A>The surface opposite of the normal vector for a triangle is not rendered.

Q>What are the two fundamental steps in texture mapping?
A>1. Assign texture coordinates to the vertices. 2. Assigning color to a pixel based on texture coordinates. 

Q>A picture element is called a pixel. What is a texture element called?
A>Texel

Q>Is color assignment in texture mapping performed in the vertex shader or fragment shader?
A>Fragment shader.

Q>What is minification?
A>Many texels to one pixel

Q>What is magnification?
A>One texel to many pixels

Q>What is mipmapping and why is it used?
A>Store a series of progressively smaller pre-filtered copies of the same texture. Used to reduce aliasing.

Q>How is the appeareance of a bump achieved in bump mapping?
A>The bump map changes the surface normal at each pixel before lighting calculations without changing geoemtry.

Q>What is a bump map?
A>Is a grayscale texture where each texel's intensity represents a height value on a surface.

Q>What is NPR an acronym for?
A>Non-Photorealistic Rendering

Q>Give an example of a method to assign texture coordinates to a vector.
A>UV unwraping, planar projection, cylindrical project, box/cubic projection.

Q>What type of edges are outlined in cel shading to produce a cartoonish apperance.
A>Silhouette edges are outlined or could be crease edges or border edges.

Q>Name three buffers in the set of framebuffers.
A>front buffer, back buffer, depth buffer, stencil buffer.

Q>What are the two cases in Cohen-Sutherland to define polygons in or outside the the viewing volume?
A>Trivial accept and trivial reject.

Q>Why is ray tracing called a global illumination model?
A>Accounts for indirect lighting, not just direct light from a source hitting a surface.

Q>When does ray tracing produce the same image as the phong illumination model?
A>Scene contains only diffuse, non-reflective, non-refractive, opaque surfaces. When there is no recursion.

Q>What is a shadow feeler?
A>A shadow feeler (shadow ray) is a ray cast from the surface intersection point towards a light source during ray tracing.

Q>Transformations that take the prototype to the desired position in model spce is called an ____ transformation.
A>Instance transformation

Q>When drawing the torso of a figure in a hierarchical model in what order do you multiply scale and translation, what is the ModelView matrix?
A>M_s * M_i, put into place then set up the camera.

A>Triangle mesh
